GRAMMAR NOTES

1 Can

Can has different meanings. We use can to talk about ability and possibility.

Example (ability): I can understand Korean.

Example (possibility): I can meet you at 4:00.

Can comes before the verb. The verb is always in the base form.

Example: He can dance.

BE CAREFUL! Do not use to after can. Do not add -s or -ing to verbs that follow can.

CORRECT: He can speak English.

NOT: He can to speak English.

NOT: He can speaks English.

NOT: He can speaking English.

2 Can't

The negative of can is can't.

Example: I can't understand you.

USAGE NOTE: Cannot is a form of the negative. It is not very common in speaking.

Example: I cannot understand you.

3 Yes/No Questions and Answers

For yes/no questions, put can before the subject. We use short answers in speaking.

Q: Can they speak Russian?

A: Yes, they can. or Yes.

Q: Can she write well?

A: No, she can't. or No.

4 Wh- Questions and Answers

For most wh- questions, use a wh- question word + can + the subject + the base form of the verb.

Q: When can you work?

A: I can work in the morning.

For questions about the subject, use a wh- question word + can + the base form of the verb.

Q: Who can sing?

A: Rosa can sing. or Rosa can.

PRONUNCIATION NOTE: Stress on Can and Can't

When can comes before a base form of the verb, we usually pronounce it /kən/ or /kn/ and stress the verb.
Example: She can SING.

When can't comes before a base form of the verb, we usually pronounce it /kænt/ and stress both can't and the verb.
Example: She CAN'T SING.
