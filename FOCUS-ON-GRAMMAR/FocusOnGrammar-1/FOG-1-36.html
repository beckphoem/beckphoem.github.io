GUARD: They're on the second floor. We're on the first floor.

MARINA: OK.

GUARD: So take the elevator or the stairs. The elevator is on your left, behind the gift shop. The stairs are on your right, in back of the information booth. Then go up one flight. The African Masks are just up the stairs. They're between the sculpture and the Chinese garden.

GUARD: The restroom?

GRAMMAR NOTES

1. Prepositions of place tell where something is.

  • Common prepositions: in, in front of, in back of, on, on your right, on your left, behind, between, near, next to, under.
  • Example: My bag is under my seat.
  • Example: Your umbrella is near the door.

2. For prepositions in addresses, use:

  • in before a country, a state, a province, a city, a room number. (He's in Canada. He's in room 302.)
  • on before a street, an avenue, a road. (It's on Main Street.)
  • at before a building number. (We're at 78 Main Street.)
  • on the before a floor. (We're on the second floor.)
  • on the corner of before a street or streets. (It's on the corner of Main Street and Mott Avenue.)

3. For prepositions before places use:

  • in school or at school (I'm in school from 9 to 11. I'm at school now.)
  • at work (She's at work right now.)
  • at home or home (No one is at home. OR No one is home.)
