STEP 2: GRAMMAR PRESENTATION

PRESENT OF BE: YES / NO QUESTIONS AND WH- QUESTIONS

Yes / No Questions & Short Answers (Singular)

Be Subject Yes No
Am I in room 2? Yes, you are. No, you're not. / you aren't.
Are you I am. I'm not.
Is he / she / it he is. / she is. / it is. he's not. / he isn't.
she's not. / she isn't.
it's not. / it isn't.

Yes / No Questions & Short Answers (Plural)

Be Subject Yes No
Are we on time? Yes, you are. No, you're not. / you aren't.
you we are. we're not. / we aren't.
they they are. they're not. / they aren't.

Wh- Questions

Wh-Word Be Subject Short Answers Long Answers
Where are you from? Michigan. I'm from Michigan.
Why here? I'm the teacher. I'm here because I'm the teacher.
What is your name? Alex. My name is Alex.
How he? Good. He's a good teacher.
Who she? A student. She's a student.

GRAMMAR NOTES

  1. Yes / no questions usually have a yes or no answer.
    A: Is he a student?
    B: Yes, he is. OR No, he isn't.
  2. Wh- questions ask for information.
    A: Who is he?
    B: He's the teacher.
  3. Use am, is, or are before a subject in yes / no questions.
    BE - SUBJECT
    • Am I in English 2?
    • Is she a student?
    • Are we late?
  4. We usually answer yes / no questions with short answers, but we can also give long answers.
    BE CAREFUL! Do not use contractions in short answers with yes.
    A: Are you a new student?
    B: Yes. OR Yes, I am. OR Yes, I am a new student.
    NOT: Yes, I'm.
  5. Wh- questions ask about the following:
    Who → people | What → things | Where → places | When → time | How → in what way
    Why asks for a reason. We can give short or long answers. In long answers, use because before the reason.
    • Who are they?
    • What is in your bag?
    • A: Why are you late?
    B: My bus was late. OR I'm late because my bus was late.
  6. Use a wh- word before is or are in wh- questions.
    WH- WORD - BE
    • What is your name?
    • Where are the books?
  7. You can use contractions (short forms) with wh- words in speaking and informal writing. We usually don't write the contraction with are.
    • Where's the library?
    • When's the class trip?
    SPOKEN ONLY: Where're the kids?
  8. We usually give short answers to wh- questions. We can also give long answers.
    A: Where are you from?
    B: Mexico. OR I'm from Mexico.
  9. When we do not know the answer to a question, we say, "I don't know."
    A: Is the men's room nearby?
    B: I don't know.
  10. When someone asks a yes / no question that we think is true, we say, "Yes, I think so." If we think it is not true, we can say, "No, I don't think so."
    A: Is she in the library?
    B: Yes, I think so. OR No, I don't think so.

WRITING PRACTICE

Sample Paragraph

Dưới đây là một đoạn văn mẫu sử dụng các cấu trúc câu hỏi và câu trả lời đã học. Hãy đọc kỹ.

My name is Carlos. I am a new student in this city. My first day of school is today. I am a little nervous. Is my teacher nice? Are the other students friendly? My classroom is in room 204. Where is that? Oh, it's right here. The class is full. I hope I am on time.
