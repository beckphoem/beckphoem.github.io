MARK: Is the restaurant near here? I'm hungry.

STEVE: Yes, it is.

MARK: Is it good?

STEVE: Don't worry. It's very good. It's Indian food.

MARK: Great! I love Indian food.

STEVE: Now drive to the corner and turn left at Jackson Street.

MARK: At the gas station?

STEVE: Yes. Then go two blocks on Jackson.

MARK: Got it.¹

STEVE: OK. Turn right at the next corner. At Third Avenue?

MARK: Yes. The restaurant is on the corner on your right.

STEVE: Is that it?

MARK: Yes, it is. Don't park here. It's a bus stop. Park behind the truck.

STEVE: OK. Please hand me² my jacket. ... Uh, wait a second. ... Steve? The restaurant is empty.

MARK: Really? It's usually packed.³

STEVE: Is that a sign on the door?

MARK: Uh-huh. ... Closed for vacation.

¹ got it: I understand | ² hand me: give me | ³ packed: full of people

STEP 2: GRAMMAR PRESENTATION

IMPERATIVES

Affirmative Negative
Turn left. Don't turn right.
Park here. Don't park there.

GRAMMAR NOTES

1. Imperative

Use the imperative for directions, instructions, requests, and suggestions.

  • Turn left. (direction)
  • Answer the questions. (instruction)
  • Please hand me my jacket. (request)
  • Try this cake. (suggestion)

Use the base form of the verb for the imperative.

  • Open the door.
USAGE NOTE: Please makes a request more polite. Please comes at the beginning or the end of the sentence.
Please help me. / Help me, please.

2. Negative Form of the Imperative

Use do not + the base form for the negative.

  • Do not park here.

Don't is the short form (contraction) of do not.

  • Don't park here.
