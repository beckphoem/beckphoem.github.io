GRAMMAR NOTES

1 Was and Were

The past of be has two forms: was and were. Use was with I, he, she, and it. Use were with you, we, and they.

I was at a movie last night.

The girls were at the library yesterday.

They were at the library yesterday.

2 Negative Statements

Use was or were + not to make negative statements.

We often use the contractions wasn't and weren't in speaking and informal writing.

I was not alone.

I wasn't asleep.

You were not at home.

You weren't awake.

3 Yes/No Questions

To ask a yes/no question, put was or were before the subject.

Was SUBJECT
Was the movie interesting?
Were you alone at the movie?

4 Short Answers

You can use a pronoun and was, wasn't, were, or weren't in short answers.

You can also just answer yes or no, and then give more information.

A: Was Mary at the library yesterday?

B: Yes, she was.

A: Were your friends at home last night?

B: No, they were not at a concert.
