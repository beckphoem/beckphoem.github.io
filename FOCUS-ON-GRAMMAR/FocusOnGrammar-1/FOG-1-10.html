GRAMMAR NOTES

1. Uses of the Simple Present

Use the simple present to talk about facts and things that happen again and again.

  • I live in Redmond. (a fact)
  • Everyone needs food and water. (a fact)
  • He studies every evening. (a thing that happens again and again)

2. Affirmative Statements

In affirmative statements, use the base form of the verb with I, you, we, and they.

The verb changes with he, she, and it:

  • Add -s to most verbs. (He needs help.)
  • Add -ies to verbs that end in a consonant + y. (She studies hard.)
  • Add -es to verbs that end in -ch, -o, -ss, -x, -s, or -z. (Jomo goes to the university every day.)

3. Negative Statements

In negative statements, use do not + the base form of the verb with I, you, we, and they.

Use does not + the base form of the verb with he, she, and it.

We often use the contractions don't and doesn't in speaking and informal writing.

4. Be and Have

Be and have are irregular verbs.

  • I am a student. Elena is a student, too.
  • I have a lot of friends. Bi-Yun has a lot of friends, too.
