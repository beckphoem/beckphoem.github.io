GRAMMAR NOTES

1. Nouns

A noun is the word for a person, animal, place, or thing.

Nouns are singular or plural.
Singular means one.
Plural means more than one.

2. Singular Nouns

A and an come before singular nouns.

Use a before a word that begins with a consonant sound. (e.g., This is a fork.)

Use an before a word that begins with a vowel sound. (e.g., This is an egg.)

3. Plural Nouns

Add -s to most nouns to make them plural. (e.g., spoon → spoons)

Add -es to nouns that end in s, ss, z, ch, sh, and x. (e.g., glass → glasses)

BE CAREFUL! Do not put a or an before plural nouns.

5. Proper Nouns

Proper nouns are the names of people and places on the map. They start with a capital letter.

BE CAREFUL! Do not put a or an before proper nouns.
