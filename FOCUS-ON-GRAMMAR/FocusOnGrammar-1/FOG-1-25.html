GRAMMAR NOTES

1 Irregular Verbs

Irregular verbs have different forms in the simple past.

Here are several common irregular verbs and their past forms.

  • become → became
  • do → did
  • grow → grew
  • say → said
  • see → saw
  • send → sent
  • sit → sat
  • take → took
  • win → won
  • write → wrote

Jaime became a teacher.
I did a lot of work yesterday.

Remember that the simple past forms of be are was, wasn't, were, and weren't.

She was born in Mexico.
We were born in Canada.

2 Negative Forms of Simple Past Verbs

To make negative sentences with both regular and irregular verbs in the simple past, use did not + the base form of the verb.

He did not eat much.

Use the contraction didn't + the base form in conversation and informal writing.

He didn't eat much.

BE CAREFUL!

Don't use did or didn't with the past form of the verb. Use the base form.

He didn't have breakfast.
NOT He didn't had breakfast.

3 Yes/No Questions and Short Answers in the Simple Past

To make yes/no questions in the simple past, use did + the subject + the base form of the verb.

Did you stop for lunch?

You can use did or didn't in the short answer to a question in the simple past. You can also use just the word yes or no.

A: Did it rain?
B: Yes, it did. or Yes.
    No, it didn't. or No.
