STEP 2: GRAMMAR PRESENTATION

SIMPLE PRESENT: YES/NO QUESTIONS

Yes/No Questions with DO

Yes/No Questions Short Answers (Affirmative) Short Answers (Negative)
Do I have your number? Yes, you do. No, you don't.
Do you like parties? Yes, I do. No, I don't.
Do we have a day off? Yes, we do. No, we don't.
Do they have new friends? Yes, they do. No, they don't.

Yes/No Questions with DOES

Yes/No Questions Short Answers (Affirmative) Short Answers (Negative)
Does she wear red? Yes, she does. No, she doesn't.
Does he have a day off? Yes, he does. No, he doesn't.
Does that word mean "yes"? Yes, it does. No, it doesn't.

GRAMMAR NOTES

1. Yes/No Questions

Use do or does + a subject + the base form of the verb to ask yes/no questions in the simple present.

  • Use do with I, you, we, and they. (e.g., Do you like questionnaires?)
  • Use does with he, she, and it. (e.g., Does she like the color red?)

2. Short Answers

We usually use short answers in conversation. (e.g., A: Does he have a day off? B: Yes, he does.)

Sometimes we use long answers. (e.g., A: Do they stay home on Fridays? B: Yes. They stay home on Fridays.)
