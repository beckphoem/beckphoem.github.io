GRAMMAR NOTES

1. There is / There are

There is and There are introduce people or things into a conversation or a piece of writing.

  • We use there is to tell about the existence of: a person, a thing.
    Example: There is a salesperson near the door. There is a shoe store at the mall.
  • We use there are to tell about the existence of: people, things.
    Example: There are two women in the store. There are restaurants on the third level.
We often use there are to tell about the number of people or things that exist.
Example: There are 14,000 parking spaces at the mall.
It is very common to use the contraction there's with singular nouns. However, we almost never use the contraction there're with plural nouns.
Example: There's a salesperson near the door.
NOT: There're two women in the store.
2. There is / There are for Location or Time

We often use there is or there are to tell the location of people or things or the time of events.

  • location: There's an aquarium on the first floor.
  • time: There are concerts on Friday and Saturday nights.
3. Negative Forms

In the negative, use isn't and aren't.

There isn't a school in the mall.

There aren't any malls near our home.

We don't often use the full forms there is not or there are not.
4. Yes/No Questions

Begin yes/no questions with Is there or Are there.

Is there a mall nearby?

5. Agreement

When two nouns follow there is or there are, the verb usually agrees with the first noun.

There's a shoe store and two hair salons.

There are two hair salons and a shoe store.

6. There is and It is...; There are and They are

We often use there is or there are the first time we introduce people or things. We use he, she, it, or they to tell more about the people or things.

There's a Thai restaurant across the street. It's next to the shoe store.

There are three little girls in the dress store. They're excited about all the beautiful dresses.

WRITING PRACTICE

Đây là một đoạn văn mẫu về một địa điểm mua sắm. Sau khi đọc, hãy nhấn nút bên dưới để xem cách các câu được tách ra.

My Favorite Bookstore

There is a wonderful little bookstore in my neighborhood called "The Reading Nook." It's not a big store, but it's full of interesting books. There are shelves packed with novels, history books, and even a special section for local authors. There's a cozy armchair in the corner where you can sit and read. The owner, Mr. Harrison, is very friendly. He's always ready with a good book recommendation. There aren't any loud noises or crowds, so it's a perfect place to relax. I love going there on Saturday afternoons.
