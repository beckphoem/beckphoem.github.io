GRAMMAR NOTE 1: The Simple Past: Meaning

Use the simple past to tell about something that started and finished at a specific time in the past.

Example: They visited Rio de Janeiro last year.

GRAMMAR NOTE 2: The Simple Past: Forms

There are three endings for regular simple past verbs in affirmative statements: -ed, -d, and -ied.

  • -ed   We landed in Caracas. (past of land)
  • -d   I arrived late. (past of arrive)
  • -ied   He tried to change his flight. (past of try)

The verb form does not change. It is the same for all subjects.

  • I visited Seoul in 2015.
  • She visited México City two years ago.
  • They visited Madrid last year.
  • Karen visited Rio de Janeiro last month.

GRAMMAR NOTE 3: The Simple Past: Negative Statements

Use did not + the base form of the verb in negative statements.

Example: We did not stay at a hotel.

USAGE NOTE: Use didn't for negative statements in speaking or informal writing.

Example: We didn't stay at a hotel.

GRAMMAR NOTE 4: Past Time Markers

We often use the simple past with past time markers. These are phrases that tell when something happened in the past.

Time markers usually come at the beginning or the end of a sentence.

Examples:

  • She arrived here two months ago.
  • I lived in Colombia last year.
  • I was in Miami yesterday.
  • Last night, we watched a travel show.

BE CAREFUL! Use ago after a length of time. Do not use before + a length of time.

✓ I visited my uncle one month ago.
✗ I visited my uncle before one month.

BE CAREFUL! We say last night, not yesterday night.

✓ I visited my cousin last night.
✗ I visited my cousin yesterday night.

PRONUNCIATION NOTE: Regular Past Tense Endings

Regular simple past verbs end in three sounds: /d/, /t/, or /ɪd/.

  • /d/   He arrived late.
  • /t/   They worked at a hotel.
  • /ɪd/   She waited for him at the airport.
